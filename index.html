Hard gate. If the environment is wrong, no trade. This layer decides whether risk is worth taking at all.

Layer 3 — Setup Quality

Soft gate. This layer does not reject the idea forever, but it controls timing quality and whether you should wait for a better setup.

Soft Gate

Setup Type

What Broad setup family you are trading.
Ideal Trend pullback or breakout continuation. Loose structure means wait.
Tag Setup classification

Pattern Quality

What Cleanliness of the visible chart structure.
Ideal Clear base or orderly pullback. Imperfect can still be watchlist-worthy. Messy means wait.
Tag Pattern clarity

RSI Context

What RSI posture relative to setup type.
Ideal Supportive pullback or reset. Neutral means wait for improvement. Unsupportive is weak timing.
Tag Momentum context

Bollinger / Location

What Price location within the setup.
Ideal Good location near support/reset. Acceptable is not ideal. Poor location means chasing.
Tag Entry quality filter
Locked
If setup is weak, wait. This is a timing-quality layer, not a permanent reject layer unless setup is clearly invalid.

Layer 4 — Entry Trigger

Soft / wait gate. Never enter on setup alone. You want the actual trigger candle and alignment.

Wait Gate

Trigger Candle

What Actual price-action trigger at the decision point.
Ideal Confirmed trigger only. Developing is wait. Rejected candle means step back.
Tag Execution trigger

Volume Confirmation

What Participation level behind the move.
Ideal Volume should support the break or reversal. Average is okay. Weak is suspect.
Tag Participation check

Multi-Timeframe Alignment

What Whether the timeframes are pointing in the same direction.
Ideal All 3 aligned is best. Monthly + weekly only means wait for daily trigger.
Tag Alignment filter

Market Support at Entry

What Broad market tone at the exact entry moment.
Ideal Supportive tape is best. Neutral means caution. Against entry means wait.
Tag Execution environment
Locked
If trigger is not there yet, wait. Valid idea does not mean valid entry yet.

Layer 5 — Risk / Reward

Hard gate. If the math is bad, reject the trade no matter how good the chart looks.

Hard Gate

Reward-to-Risk

What Whether the trade math supports the risk taken.
Ideal At least 2:1. Near threshold is caution. Below 2:1 is reject.
Tag Expectancy gate

Stop Quality

What Quality of stop placement.
Ideal Structural and volatility-aware. Needs work means refine. Arbitrary is not allowed.
Tag Risk control

Target Quality

What Clarity of the upside objective.
Ideal Clear target from structure, range, or measured move. Approximate is caution.
Tag Exit planning

Tradeability / Liquidity

What Ease of getting in and out efficiently.
Ideal Good liquidity only. Tradable but not ideal reduces quality. Poor is reject.
Tag Execution practicality

Level 2 50 MA Position Weekly price relative to 50 MA Price above 50 MA Near / testing Price below 50 MA Above = trend intact. Near/testing = acceptable only if structure still holds. Below = weakening.

Level 4 Volume Confirmation Does volume support the move? Supports move Average Weak / contradictory Stronger volume improves breakout and reversal quality. Weak volume lowers confidence.
Level 4 Multi-Timeframe Alignment Monthly, weekly, daily alignment Monthly + weekly + daily Monthly + weekly only Misaligned Weekly gives permission. Daily gives execution. Daily should not fight weekly.
Level 4 Market Support at Entry Broad tape at the moment of entry Supportive Neutral Against entry If the market is breaking down hard, even good setups often fail.
Level 5 Reward-to-Risk Math of upside vs downside ≥ 2:1 Near threshold < 2:1 If the math is poor, reject the trade even if the chart looks attractive.

Level 5 Target Quality Is there a clear upside objective? Clear target Approximate Unclear Use resistance, measured moves, or range projections. Vague targets reduce decision quality.
Level 5 Tradeability / Liquidity Can you enter and exit efficiently? Good Tradable but not ideal Poor Good liquidity and tighter spreads improve real execution quality.
Quick rule: Layer 1 + Layer 2 must pass first. Layer 3 and Layer 4 control timing quality. Layer 5 decides if the trade is worth taking at all.
